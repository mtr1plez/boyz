"SOLUTION" FILM REVIEW

BY THE BOYZ(NO KANA)

LET'S GET STARTED

Film Description

Two detectives from the boss of Sammy Jr come across a strange case about the kidnapping of a certain Emily Thompson, which they will have to solve and find the criminal. Throughout the film, detectives Orynkhan and Asem are trying to get on the trail of the kidnapper and do everything right until the evidence leads them to an unexpected result...

STYLE

The film's style follows the best traditions of the noir detective story. Darkened rooms, black and white visuals, leisurely storytelling and attention to detail. The script, apparently written by Medina, was deliberately aimed at a noir thriller, and Kanat's direction allowed the idea to turn into a serviceable execution. Kanat acted as both director and cameraman. His work shows a love for medium and detailed shots (notably, there are absolutely no wide or long shots in the film), a static camera and experiments with light. Kanat used a single light source, which adds a noir feel to the film.

Another interesting tool in the film is command-text. The text acts as a kind of explanation of history, despite the fact that the essence is clear and visual. The text also lets us know that the detectives have entered a new stage of the investigation. In the first case, text can be your worst enemy, devaluing visual storytelling, because why show action when you can do it in text? And in the second case, the text can be a friend and help give structure to the film, which is what it does
